About Alibi

Alibi is a new sort of magazine focused on literature and rock & roll. It’s published using a mix of traditional and innovative approaches – not only online, not only print, but a unique blend of the page, the stage, and the web. No high-quality magazine exists that presents rock music and literature side-by-side and explores their exhilarating relationship. Not until now.

Live Events

Alibi will present marquee live shows with the release of each quarterly issue, and our first event this winter will be headlined by Neko Case and a writer we’ll announce soon. Each Alibi event will feature two marquee guests – an author and a musician.  The shows will include live interviews, performances, and collaborations.  The first few events will take place in the San Francisco Bay Area.

Alibi on the Page

The print magazine will appear quarterly, and our first issue will feature new writing by by Tobias Wolff, Jim White, Zach Rogue, Kim Addonizio, Blake Hazard, Ted Gioia, Adrian McKinty, Viva Las Vegas, and many others.  The magazine will contain interviews, short stories, essays, and a handful of features in which musicians write about books and authors write about music.

Alibi on the Web: Film, Audio, and Extras

The first Alibi mini-concert, a film featuring Zach from Rogue Wave, will be posted to the Alibi site soon, as well as brand new columns by Jim White and Viva Las Vegas.  The Alibi website will offer a series of mini-concerts and mini-readings, as well as columns, blogs, reviews, new songs, stories read aloud, interviews, and conversations.  Some of the content will be free to everyone, and some will be exclusively for subscribers.

Do It for the Kids

Education is important to us here at Alibi. We plan to give copies of the magazine to schools and offer free student matinees of our events.  If we can inspire a few punks to grow up to be the next Tom Waits or Cormac McCarthy – or at least if we can light some sparks – that ain’t a bad thing.

Board of Directors

Alibi is a registered nonprofit corporation in California.  Our board of directors has five members:

Dan Stone is a writer, bartender, and the editor and Executive Director of Alibi. He has taught middle school, high school, and college, and also worked for six years as a program manager and documentary producer at the National Endowment for the Arts. Stone has published prose, poetry, and translations from Italian, and has an MFA in fiction from Boston University.

Neko Case is an award-winning musician who has released several critically acclaimed recordings and performed all over the world. Her most recent album, Middle Cyclone, debuted at #3 on the Billboard charts and was rated the number one album of 2009 by Amazon.

Zach Rogue has for eight long years written, performed, and produced music for the indie-rock band Rogue Wave, touring the world and releasing four full-length albums. A solo musical project called Release the Sunbird appeared in July 2011. Before turning to music full-time, he worked several tech jobs and earned a degree in Political Science from UC Davis.

Brandon Herring is co-founder and Design Director of 17FEET, an award-winning web-design boutique in San Francisco, CA. His clients range from Fortune 500 companies to Bay Area start-ups and nonprofits. He has also served as Art Director for the San Francisco Independent Film Festival. His work for the festival has been featured in numerous design publications.

Jake Martin has worked for nine years at Pixar Animation Studios in Emeryville, CA. He is credited on four Academy Award-winning feature films, and most recently served as Production Manager for Cars 2. He earned a B.A. in English from Colorado College.
